## 2. General Mixing Directives

The mix must prioritize **depth, clarity, and rhythmic impact** while maintaining the **Epic Deep** atmosphere.

| Element Group | Mixing Directive | Key Frequency Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Kick & Sub-Bass** | **Mono** below 120 Hz. Kick transient focused on 60-80 Hz. Sub-bass sustained and side-chained heavily to the kick (Ratio 4:1, Fast Attack/Release). | **Sub-Bass:** 30-60 Hz. **Kick:** 60-80 Hz (Punch). |
| **Latin Percussion** | **Wide Stereo Field.** Use panning and stereo delay to create a "wall of rhythm." Congas/Bongos should occupy the mid-low range (150-400 Hz) for warmth. | **Congas/Bongos:** 150-400 Hz (Warmth). **Timbales/Cowbell:** 5-8 kHz (Cut/Clarity). |
| **Pads & Melodies** | **Wide Stereo Field.** Pads should be layered and occupy the background, heavily reverbed. Leads (Trumpet, Supersaw) should be brought forward in the mix during drops. | **Pads:** 800 Hz - 3 kHz (Air/Lushness). **Leads:** 3-5 kHz (Presence). |
| **Vocals & FX** | **Dry/Wet Contrast.** Use dry, upfront vocals for rhythmic stabs (Movement II) and heavily reverbed, delayed vocals for atmospheric texture (Movement I & IV). | **Rhythmic Vocals:** 1-3 kHz (Clarity). **Atmospheric Vocals:** Heavy Reverb/Delay Tails. |

## 3. Mastering Directives

The final master must be loud and competitive with modern techno and house tracks, but with a focus on preserving the dynamic range of the percussion and the depth of the sub-bass.

1.  **Limiting:** Target an integrated LUFS of **-8 to -7 LUFS**. Use a transparent limiter to achieve loudness without audible pumping or distortion.
2.  **Stereo Widening:** Apply subtle stereo widening (e.g., Mid/Side processing) to the high-frequency content (above 4 kHz) to enhance the **Epic** feel, but keep the low-end strictly mono.
3.  **Dynamic Control:** Use a multi-band compressor to gently control the low-mids (200-500 Hz) to prevent muddiness, ensuring the Latin percussion remains punchy and clear.
4.  **Final Polish:** A final high-shelf EQ boost (0.5 dB at 12 kHz) to add "air" and sparkle to the hi-hats and shakers.
